Richard's Timeline

  • Stallman launched the GNU Project in September 1983 to create a Unix-like computer operating system composed entirely of free software.
  • Stallman pioneered the concept of copyleft, which uses the principles of copyright law to preserve the right to use
  • In 1989, he co-founded the League for Programming Freedom.
  • American free software movement activist and programmer.
  • In 1999 Stallman published “The Free Universal Encyclopedia and Learning Resource,” a paper calling for the creation of an open-source encyclopaedia.
  • In September 2019 Stallman attracted controversy after attempting to discredit an alleged victim of Jeffrey Epstein, a convicted sex offender.

“With software there are only two possibilites: either the users control the programme or the programme controls the users. If the programme controls the users, and the developer controls the programme, then the programme is an instrument of unjust power.”
